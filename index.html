Welcome to the John Stott Memorial Birding Day 2021
8th May 2021 #johnstottbirdingday

The day

‘The ‘John Stott memorial birding day’ is a global one-day event on 8 May 2021 to commemorate, celebrate and continue John Stott’s legacy as a theologian, pastor and birdwatcher in his centenary year.

Whether you are a novice or an expert birder, we invite you join friends old and new to head into the great outdoors and enjoy the avian part of God’s good creation.

How you can take part

The organizers

The ‘John Stott memorial birding day’ is brought to you by A Rocha, a family of organizations around the world working in community-based nature conservation. Founded in 1983, we believe the care of creation belongs at the heart of our calling as followers of Christ. John Stott was a generous and encouraging friend to A Rocha from its earliest days; his influence continues to be felt in our culture, theology and values today.

To find out more about A Rocha, please visit www.arocha.org

The Man

John Stott was at the forefront of bringing creation care to the attention of the global Church. He cared deeply for the plight of the planet because he was hardwired with a love of nature in general of birds in particular. But he was also someone who listened carefully to the Bible and he heard within it God’s great love and concern for all he had made, not just the humans.

He particularly loved Psalm 104, which he said was the first great ecological poem. On the tenth anniversary of A Rocha in 1993, he preached a sermon which cited many psalms, but one reference which particularly struck us was Psalm 105: 1, ‘Give thanks to the Lord. Call on his name and make known to the nations what he has done. Sing praise to him and tell of all his wonderful acts.’ And he said, ‘Of course we’re used to the idea that we’re to bear witness to what God has done in Jesus for the salvation of the world. But the Scripture says we are also to bear witness to the wonderful works of our creator. God’s mighty acts in creation and redemption are to be made known throughout the world. I hope, Sisters and Brothers, that we will not be afraid to bear witness to the creator as well as to the redeemer.’

29 April is the centenary of John Stott’s birth. Although he didn’t live to celebrate his 100th birthday with us, his legacy endures. We hope you will enjoy this day of birding in his memory.

The Bird Race.

You can compete as an individual or a team. Winners of each individual category will be awarded a bird book from John Stott’s own collection.

We are partnering with eBird’s Global Big Day. To take part in the competition, you will need to create an eBird account, submit your sightings with the eBird website or with their mobile app. and To participate to the competition, simply share your submitted checklist with the username johnstottbirdingday .

On the day, please log your data which will then appear on this page in real time so you can see your ranking.

The Auction

All proceeds from this auction will go towards furthering A Rocha’s work around the world. If you have a painting or book given to you by John Stott that you would like to donate, please contact international@arocha.org with details.

The retreat

If a day staring at the sky through binoculars or crouched in a bush with a telescope doesn’t appeal, use 8 May as an opportunity to take some time out with God and learn more about him from ‘the birds our teachers,’ as the title of one of John’s books called them?

We’ve put together some readings, prayers and reflections drawn from John’s writings, and invite you to head outside alone or with a small group of friends to connect with God through the beauty of all he has made.
